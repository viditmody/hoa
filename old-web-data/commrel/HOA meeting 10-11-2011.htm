COMMITTEE REPORTS

 

 

Finance Committee:

 

The operating account looks good. However, we know that historically, several big invoices hit at the end of the year. Next meeting will be Monday, October 24 at 6:30 p.m. in the north clubhouse. Everyone is invited and urged to attend.

 

 

Social Committee:

We held our first “morning coffee” last Friday. We plan to host them on the first Friday of every month.  The “gold party” was held earlier today. The committee will hand out goodie bags to trick-or-treaters on Monday, Oct. 31 at 4:30 p.m.

 

 

 

 

Manager’s Report:

 

 

It was reported last month that there was a Bldg. 7 patio where the tiles were popping up. Well, it was not the patio tiles that were affected, but tiles inside the unit, so the HOA is not involved.

 

Metro Hardscape has finished what they were able to do regarding the grinding of sidewalks to eliminate tripping hazards.

 

Allied Trustee Services refunded $2,400 to the HOA due to an error on their behalf.

 

A representative of Zenith Ins. will come this Thursday to do a workers’ comp. audit.

 

Maintenance man, Antonio, did a walk through with All Seasons to inspect the roofs after the “elastomeric” roof coating project was completed. He identified 14 spots were All Seasons needed to make repairs/ adjustments.

 

Serpico is aware of the concerns regarding the oleander flowers that fall on the red tile. Several vendors are helping with this situation.

 

A representative of Socher Insurance has been invited to the next HOA meeting to explain about the different earthquake insurance alternatives and to answer any insurance question from the attendees.

 

 

 

OPEN FORUM:

 

Residents saw a note left on the bulletin board of Bldg. 5 stating that a neighbor knew who was littering around that area and that if did not stop, the office/board would be notified.

 

 

A young man was going around knocking on doors soliciting magazine subscriptions. The newsletter will remind people of the no soliciting rule.
