New Board Members Wanted…You Can Make Murieta Marvelous!

 

As your outgoing Board President, I would like to extend a personal request to you to consider becoming a Board Member. Each year in the Spring, the homeowners of Murieta vote for fellow homeowners to make up the 5 member Board of Directors who then serve for one year. The Board oversees the management of the community through our onsite manager and staff, decides how the dues are spent and makes and enforces the community rules at the Murieta.

Being a Board Member takes only one evening per month for the monthly Board meeting, requires no prior or specialized experience, only common sense and a willingness to "give back" a little to our community. Since the position is volunteer and unpaid, there is no liability risk to you. Our community benefits from new people getting involved because you bring a fresh perspective on how the community is managed, come up with new ideas and new solutions to the issues that face us. The end result is a better quality of life for all residents and improved property values for the important investment in our homes.

In recent years and due to the efforts of several Board Members, the financial situation of Murieta has improved dramatically. The Association is now appropriately funded, and we have begun to make several noticeable improvements to the grounds and facilities. In the near future, decisions will need to be made on replacing the carpeting inside the buildings, among other projects. Wouldn’t you like to be "in on the process" and have a hand in directly voting on these and other issues?

I have been involved in Murieta management and improvement projects for 2 years and I can recommend it highly. It has been a wonderful learning experience. The satisfaction of providing a service to our fellow community members has been a great reward. I urge you to consider volunteering just one night per month for one year. You’ll be glad you did.

 

Don Jones, President

 

 

Why to become a member of the Murieta Board of Directors?

 

The most important asset that most of us have is our home. When you live in a condominium complex, the entire campus is part of your home. No matter what your individual unit looks like, a prospective buyer will look at the entire complex before making a buying decision. This is where the board of directors plays an important role in the value of your home.

The board is responsible for overseeing the maintenance and operations of the association’s Common Areas. The Murieta has been very fortunate over the past 4-5 years in that we have been able to attract very responsible and committed board members.

Here shortly we will be electing a new board, so now is the time for you as a homeowner to step forward and help protect the value of your asset by becoming involved in its operations.

Being a board member does not take a lot of time or effort, but is does take a commitment for 1 year without monetary reward. Let me discuss with you some things that I see as necessary traits/skills for a board member.

A board member needs to like to interface with people, not control them. We are governed by state law, our CC&Rs, and our Rules. The board needs to listen to our member’s concerns and realize that there are some things we can change and others that we can’t.

The board member needs to be mentally mature, have common sense and be thick skinned. There will be times when homeowners won’t agree with decisions of the board. Board members may not even agree with the majority decisions but we need to work together throughout the year. Some of the questions posed to board will require deep thought and even though we want to initiate a change immediately the association may not be able make that change because of funding or the need to change our governing rules.

Being a board member will require a few hours of your time (probably around 4-5 hours a month), but isn’t your home worth spending that amount of time on it? During the first couple of months a new board member will need to spend a little more time reading and grasping the Association’s governing documents and state law regarding the management of all homeowner associations.

Speaking a second language would be a big plus for the board. We are a community of 324 homes, with over 1,000 people of different nationalities and languages living here. Some homeowners may be more comfortable discussing issues, they feel as important, in a language other than English and therefore may feel somewhat alienated because they perceive that no one on the board understands them.

All of the existing board members would like to see more residents run for the board so that the board continues to be exposed to new ideas and concerns, which is the only way we continue to make the Murieta a great place to live.

 

Carl Kennedy, Treasurer

 

 

With the April Annual Meeting Right Around the Corner…

 

As the New Year rings in, I pause to think of the past year. With its learning experiences, challenges and sometimes not so exciting moments, it was a good year.

I am thankful for my family and how they have grown this year. The "on the job training" of being a Mother has been an exhilarating roller coaster ride.

Among the many hats I wear, I chose to serve again on the Murieta Board of Directors. Why? Some of you may wonder why someone would "choose" to be on the Board. You may be thinking: "It takes too much time; No one cares; Residents are always complaining; Board Members are professionals with special training; and what does a Board Member do?"

With the April Annual Meeting right around the corner, now is the time to think about running for a seat on the Board of Directors.

No Time? Board meetings are once a month and run approximately 2 hours. Between the meetings, Board Members are available via email, phone or in person for urgent decisions and weekly office updates. I am so thankful for Susan and the staff at Murieta, I could not imagine serving on the Board without them.

Resident Complaints get you down? I’ll be truthful, I’m hurt when I see something negative about the work we do. I then remember it’s those comments that make us do better. I only wish those residents would get involved. Actions speak much louder than words, and if you are willing to do some research and use common sense to vote on issues that affect our community, you begin to see the positive energy in our community.

No Special Training?A board member doesn’t need it. They need a willingness to learn, ask questions and work with a group of fellow residents to keep Murieta a community worth living in.

That’s it! Do you care about your home? Do you care about your community? Do you have ideas to be heard? If so, have your name added to the ballot. You may find that next New Year you’ll be thankful you did.

 

May McMann, Vice President
