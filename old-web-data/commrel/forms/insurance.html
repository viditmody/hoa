Your Board of Directors and Susan Levin, General Manager (510-683-8794) have asked us to inform you of the coverages under your new Group Blanket Policy (in accordance with the provisions of the current California Civil Code requirements).

 

BUILDING:  “Special Form” perils coverage on the building, part of the original building specifications, and permanently attached to the building (you must insure your interior improvements and upgrades).  Your building is covered on a Replacement Cost basis, with no coinsurance penalty, with a $5,000 deductible per loss occurrence.  “Special Form” coverage perils do not include all losses and some of the exclusions are: Earthquake & Flood, revised building codes, wear and tear, damage by insects and vermin, wet and dry rot, and water leaks from your washer or appliances or leaks that have occurred over a period of time, etc. Limit:  $46,785,960   (was $44,986,500).

 

FIDELITY & FORGERY BOND: Your policy also includes a Blanket Bond to protect the Homeowners Association against the dishonesty of any director or employee regarding the Association’s personal property and money/securities/reserve funds.  Non-employees/non-directors “3rd Party” Forgery - included.  Limit: $1,000,000  (was $2,000,000)  ($10,000 deductible).

 

LIABILITY:  Public Liability protection is provided for the commonly owned areas as defined by the covenants, conditions and restrictions.  There is coverage up to $5,000,000 per occurrence under the broad liability form.  Coverage within your unit and your Personal Liability is not provided (you must provide under your individual Condominium Homeowners’ Policy HO-6).  Directors & Officers Errors and Omissions coverage is included for           $1,000,000 (was $3,000,000) per occurrence ($5,000 deductible).

 

CONSTRUCTION CODE UPGRADES: New coverage added 4-15-04 for City of Newark construction upgrades required by Building Code, after an insured loss.  Limits:  $250,000 Demolition and $500,000 Increased Cost of Construction  ($5,000 Deductible). 

 

EARTHQUAKE & FLOOD:  (NOT covered).

 

EQUIPMENT BREAKDOWN COVERAGE:  (NOT covered).

                                                                                                                                                                             

WORKERS’ COMPENSATION: Statutory limits and benefits as required by California Law, for employees.

YOU MUST PURCHASE: YOUR INDIVIDUAL CONDOMINIUM HOMEOWNERS POLICY (HO-6)

 

You are required to purchase your own personal Liability and Property (contents) and Interior Improvements coverage since the master Association policy does not provide this coverage for you.  The policy, called a Condominium Homeowners Policy (HO-6), is inexpensive and readily available.  You are responsible for all Property and Liability claims which originate in the interior of your unit.

 

We suggest that individual homeowners purchase:  “All Risk” or “Special Form” coverage perils; Replacement Cost endorsement for your interior improvements, furnishings, and floor coverings; a liability limit of at least $1,000,000; loss assessment coverage; additional living expense coverage; and an “All Risk” Improvements and Betterments endorsement for full Replacement Cost on upgrades and interior improvements (i.e. floor coverings, cabinets, fixtures, window coverings, etc.).  Call your personal insurance agent for a quotation and consultation.

 

If you rent your condominium to others, simply extend the personal liability section of your residential homeowners policy to the rental unit (also be sure to cover your entire interior, as listed above, plus cover any contents: washer/dryer, furniture, refrigerator, etc.).  If you are a tenant (renter) you should purchase an Apartment Tenant’s policy for Personal Property and Liability.

 

Protect your family and property - be sure your Smoke Detectors operate properly!  Water damage is #1 source of claims – check all plumbing connections; washing machine, toilet, dishwasher, supply hoses, water heaters, etc. (replace with steel braided or “burst-proof” hoses – estimate $15/each).
