COMMITTEE REPORTS

 

Manager’s Report:

 

Antonio, our new maintenance man, continues to save the association a lot of money. He has repaired, refurbished and/or improved the appearance of various areas of the complex. Needless to say, we are very happy with him.

 

Batteries and Bulbs will now accept our old light bulbs for recycling. This is good news since the recycling center nearby wanted to charge us $0.80 per light bulb to be recycled.

 

There are a couple of water problems brewing… We don’t know how else to explain to all home owners the crucial importance of having liability insurance!

 

Paola Berny (Common area assistant manager) just celebrated her 4th anniversary at Murieta.

 

The north side parking lot is due this year for asphalt re-surfacing. A resident pointed out that it needs it. It was done last time in 2004. Board instructed office to secure competitive bids.

 

Finance Committee Report:

 

The committee got together on Mar 16, 2010 to go over the association’s financials. The cash flow budget was discussed. Next meeting will be April 20 at 6:30 pm in the north clubhouse.

 

 

Open Forum:

 

A resident expressed that she thinks gates will not help deter break-ins.

 

A resident expressed that he would like to be present if and when the parking lot security committee meets again.

 

Annual Election:

 

Inspector of elections, Marilyn Bell reports on the vote count:

 

All incumbent board members got elected. The 2010-2011 Board of Directors includes:

                             Joy Hurst               President

                             Tim Young             Vice President

                             Karen Barry            Secretary

                             Carl Kennedy          Treasurer

                             Linh Tran               Director

 

Parking Lot Security results:

Surveillance cameras around parking lot                  2 votes

Gates at Center driveway                                      65 votes

Security guards                                                     6 votes

Do nothing, leave things as they are                      57 votes

 

Even though the gates option won, we needed 50% + 1 of the association members to vote…
