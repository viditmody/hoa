NEW BUSINESS:

 

Ø        Waste Management representatives re: monies owed by Murieta for under billing 6/1/05 – 10/01/07:

 

Representatives from Waste Management (Dave Horn- Contract Compliance Manager and Greg Ong- Pricing Manager) attended the meeting to answer questions from the Board and Murieta owners.

 

Dave Horn and Greg Ong explained that starting in 2005, the invoices Murieta received from Waste Management, reflected commercial rates instead of residential multi-family rates as the contract WM has with the City of Newark stipulates. That resulted in under billing that went on until the mistake was caught in October of 2007. The under billing amounts to over $ 45,000 with a 7% allowance for the residents that moved in or out during the period in question.  In addition, WM announced that they were raising their service fees significantly over what was budgeted for 2008.

 

The allowance for Murieta is 40 gal. of trash per unit per week. A second visit in the same week is charged at 50%.  Murieta is charged by number of units, not by the amount or size of the dumpster bins.  An owner suggested looking into buying an industrial trash compactor with the goal of reducing our costs by having trash service once a week, instead of two times per week as it currently is.

 

Several residents expressed their concern and disappointment in making Murieta pay for what was clearly Waste Management’s mistake. The Board is very concerned about the association’s financial hardship and the cash flow implications of not having enough money allocated in the operating budget for trash service this year.

 

Greg Ong asked the Board to present a payment plan proposal to Waste Management. The Board is planning to attend a City of Newark council meeting to express their disapproval of having condominium complexes, like Murieta, be charged residential rates instead of commercial rates. The Board is urging all Murieta residents to attend the meeting as well.
