Manager’s Report:

Karen Barry, who has been the Board’s secretary, for the last 6 years, has moved. The HOA was very sad to see her go. At this meeting, she was presented with a plaque as a token of the Board’s appreciation for her service on the Board.

 

The manager received a call from an “inspector” representing Bank of America. He wanted access to 3 units that are occupied. The board instructed the office not to let him in. The title holder should grant access, not the HOA.

 

Manager asked our “gates” vendor about installing “spikes” on the exit center driveway to ensure that cars do not get into the complex from the exit gate. Spikes are strongly not recommended for Murieta. They need to be accompanied by cameras which would make them very expensive, they become tripping hazards, the layout of the parking lot is not appropriate, etc. The list of reasons why spikes would not be a good idea went on and on…

 

Board wanted clarification on quorum needed for different elections:

For the record, the various quorum requirements are explained in the Bylaws, page 7, Section 4.5. For assessment increases, the quorum requirement is a majority of all members: 163 ballots submitted. To pass it would take 83 yes votes. If you decide to propose changes to the CC&Rs, that would require 108 ballots returned and 55 yes votes to pass. There is no quorum requirement for election of board members.

 

Allied Trustee services is ready to foreclose, again, on the same unit we foreclosed on in the past. This time it is bank owned and Allied assures us that the sale won’t go through and that it will only serve to make the bank pay us the dues owed.

 

A resident in Bldg. 2 cut the wires to his unit’s fire horn which compromised the fire alarm system for the whole building.  Statcomm was aware the there was a “trouble on the line” but since they didn’t know where it was coming from, inspections had to be scheduled for all the units in that building which was a tremendous inconvenience to the rest of the Building 2 residents. The HOA will bill back all the charges incurred in making the fire alarm system whole again to the unit that willfully jeopardized it.

 

Office needed some guidance from the Board regarding responsibility when leaks occur in pipes. Board instructed the office to explain to residents that asked, the difference between exclusive use and common area and that responsibility, in cases of negligence, is determined by the point of origin of the leak in question.
