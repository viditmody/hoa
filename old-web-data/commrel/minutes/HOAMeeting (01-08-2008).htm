COMMITTEE REPORTS

 

 

Office Report:

 

The office received 74 packages for residents in November. In December, the number of packages received jumped to 154.

 

The office contacted Store Front Doors to ask if there is any way to make it more difficult to disable the locking mechanism on doors. They explained that all doors with a panic bar of any sort must have a way to disable the lock.

 

Bay Alarm performed the fire alarm system annual inspection last month. They stayed pretty close to the posted schedule. They changed defective heat detectors as they went along. There were 21 no-shows in total.

 

We have heard more reports of after- hours spa usage.  The Board is thinking of either changing the spa hours or turning off the heater for the spa one hour before the current closing time of 10:30 p.m.

 

Lewis Carpet Cleaners will begin the professional cleaning of all buildings’ carpets. They will do 2 buildings per month starting with Building 2 on Jan 14 and Building 1 on Jan. 15.

 

Someone tried to break into the north clubhouse and broke the lock on one of the French doors. Armor Locks came after-hours to repair the door and install a deadbolt.

(the Board asked the office to research cost of replacing all French doors)

 

A unit in the first floor of Building 6 reported water in the master bedroom’s closet.  Bell Plumbing concluded that the water originated from a defective hose bib in the fountain courtyard.

 

Waste Management has agreed to write-off the charges imposed due to “overfilled dumpsters”. Furthermore, they are going to take Murieta off the list of properties were photographs of the dumpsters are taken.

 

RPM advised us that the light bulbs on the chandeliers are not lasting long due to their working hours. The office suggested replacing them with more efficient CFL bulbs.

 

Finance Committee Report:

 

The committee met on December 18, to go over the association’s finances.

The January meeting is scheduled for Tuesday, January 22 at 6:30 p.m. in the north clubhouse.
