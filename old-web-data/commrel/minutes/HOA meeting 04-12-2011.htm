COMMITTEE REPORTS

 

Finance Committee:

 

The association is doing a good job of spending only on health and safety issues. The board and the finance committee are keeping a very close eye on the cash flow.

 

Manager’s Report:

 

The bulky waste dumpster is back and will stay until Tuesday of next week (April 19).

 

We found out from a resident, that fluorescent bulbs (the small spiral ones) can be recycled at the local Home Depot.

 

Mr. Unlimited (carpet cleaner) is aware of some stubborn reappearing stains. He wants to try with different substances to see if he can get rid of them once and for all. He will provide us with a proposal.

 

The security fence extension is being installed in the north pool area. It looks great!

 

Someone in the south side is finding “creative” ways to keep doors open. It is imperative that any resident that sees doors or gates open, help us in closing them again and to let the office know if they know who is doing this.

 

There were a couple of wild turkeys roaming around the parking lots last week. We hope they don’t intend to make Murieta their permanent residence.

 

There was a burglary during the day on a 3rd floor unit in Building 3. This is one of the buildings where doors are constantly being left open.

 

 

OPEN FORUM:

 

The Board and attending residents discussed what can be done to deter incidents like the recent Building 3 burglary. One Building 5 resident bought an inexpensive video camera system at Costco and installed it looking towards the parking lot. Perhaps if more 1st floor residents would install cameras in their patios, the would-be criminals would think twice before burglarizing Murieta.

 

A resident commented that satellite dishes on patios and balconies are not being installed professionally and look “sloppy”. He volunteered to research how to create guidelines for residents and help the complex look better.

 

A resident inquired about the reasoning behind the rule that says that cars may not park backed-in. He commented that perhaps it is an archaic rule that is no longer necessary. Office will inquire with Socher Insurance and with the fire marshal and ask their opinion on the matter.

 

A resident asked about who can he call on weekends if the main driveway gate stays open. He was told that the emergency number for R&S Gates in on a sticker on each gate. However, only a board member can authorize an “after hours” service call.
