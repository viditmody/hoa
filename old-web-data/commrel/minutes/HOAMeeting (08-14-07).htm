OPEN FORUM:

 

Resident commented that the elevator floor in building 8 is old and passed its prime. She was wondering if perhaps it would be time to replace it.

 

A resident pointed out that there are a lot of spider webs around the bushes on the north side of the complex. She is requesting if the Board could look into having the spider webs removed by power washing them.

 

The chandelier between buildings 3 & 4 is broken and can be potentially dangerous. Office will let RPM Electric know so they can replace the broken pieces and ensure that it is safe and in working order.

 

Resident is interested in organizing an international potluck dinner and inviting all Murieta residents to participate.

 

 

 

COMMITTEE REPORTS

 

 

Office Report:

 

The office has learned from an owner at The Waterford (our neighboring condominium complex), that all its owners just received a $9,000 special assessment to fund major repairs.

 

Measures are being taken to try to stop all the vandalism acts committed lately at the pools:

  • We have installed motion sensor flood lights at the south side swimming pool and the Board is considering installing them at the north side pool as well.
  • There is a proposal to fix all pool area gates to make it more difficult for anyone to be able to reach the gate handle from the bottom or the side of a gate.

 

A light pole next to guest parking by Building 8 was knocked down during a “hit and run” car accident leaving “live” wires exposed. RPM Electric had to build a new pole. A Murieta sign was also destroyed and will be replaced.

 

The front door in Building 5 is not working properly. Store Front Doors changed the electronic panic bar thinking that it would solve the problem, only to have the door fail again 2 days later. The panic bar factory rep came to inspect it and said we had a defective panic bar. Store Front Doors still needs to replace it (again).

 

11 violations were sent to the owners renting their units who still have not provided the office with the “rider to Lease Agreement” signed by them and their lessees.

 

 

Finance Committee Report:

 

The committee met on July 21 to go over the association’s finances. The August meeting will begin the 2008 budget process. The new budget needs to be approved during the October HOA meeting and mailed out to the community in November.
