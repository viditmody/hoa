COMMITTEE REPORTS

 

Finance Committee:

 

The 2012 operating budget will be finalized during the October meeting.  Dues will go up approximately $6 per unit per month. The rates for utilities have gone up dramatically and will go up again. There are around $50,000 in collections right now. Next meeting will be Tuesday, September 20 at 6:30 p.m. in the north clubhouse. Everyone is invited and urged to attend.

 

Manager’s Report:

 

There were reports of a mosquito infestation in and around Murieta. The Alameda County Mosquito Abatement Agency came and fogged the complex. By all accounts, it worked! We will call them next Summer and try to make this a yearly appointment.

 

Last time the question of buying earthquake insurance was raised to the community was during the 2007 election. The board is considering if that question should be part of the general election next April. The Board asked the office to invite a representative from  Socher Insurance to come to the October HOA meeting to discuss the options available.

 

The fire alarm system testing and inspection has been completed in Bldgs. 1 and 2. The exception of 1 room, there were no no-shows. This is a very positive start and we hope the trend continues in all other buildings.

 

Last month the suggestion was made to try and decrease the time the driveway gates remain open when a car comes in order to minimize tailgaters.  That setting is adjusted at the gates themselves (cannot change it in the office) and is currently set at 1-2 seconds. The problem is that the gate is slow in opening and closing (1ft/second).

 

 

Social Committee:

The potluck party was great. There were 35+ people in attendance and a bunch of yummy food. There are events planned from now until the end of the year and the social committee calendar is on the website.

 

OPEN FORUM:

 

A resident stated that the bushes on Cedar Blvd. desperately need to be trimmed.

 

People are throwing trash by the front door of building 5, especially on weekends.

 

A resident asked about the little maple tree on Cedar in the south side. It was the wrong kind of maple that is why it looks so different from the others. Treeco is aware of the problem and will replace it during their next visit.

 

There has been an increase in skunk sightings around here.
