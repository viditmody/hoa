OPEN FORUM:

 

A resident reported that there seems to be people in Building 5 meeting late at night and leaving trash (food, paper cups, wrappers, etc) all around the first floor and courtyard.

 

Guest parking continues to be abused. Residents are parking in guest parking.

 

Residents will be reminded in the newsletter that every car should have a Murieta parking sticker and that the association will continue to tow cars not displaying a guest parking permit.  Residents will also be reminded that they have the right to tow cars that are illegally parked in their parking stalls.

 

 

 

 

COMMITTEE REPORTS

 

 

Office Report:

 

There are new signs by every entrance to the complex and by guest parking, showing All Ways Towing Co.’s  phone number. These signs were installed to comply with the new California towing laws.

 

Pacheco Brothers reported that lack of rain in January together with the very cold weather were the reasons for the lawns to become yellow. Now that it is raining more, all lawn areas will return to their nice, green appearance.

 

There was a burglary in Building 3 and another in Building 8. Perpetrators got inside the units through open windows. 

 

The CACM (California Association of Community Managers) attended by Susan Levin was very interesting and well worth it. The Board’s packet includes some of the most relevant information regarding reserve studies and budgeting.

 

The office recommended to the Board that Murieta have a professional reserve study done every year, instead of only every 3 years. This will be included in next month’s meeting agenda.

 

 

Finance Committee Report:

 

Last meeting was held on Tuesday, January 23, 2007. The committee revised the financial statements. The Cash flow is tight and the committee recommends that the Board be very cautious with expenditures.  All owners are welcome to join these  meetings. No accounting background is necessary.

 

Next meeting will be held on Tuesday, February 20, 2007 at 6:30 p.m. in the north clubhouse.

Ø        New Information Regarding Defibrillator:

 

Murieta’s weight room is not a “health studio” as defined by the new health and safety California code and therefore, is not required to have a defibrillator.

 

Motion:       Carl Kennedy made a motion to post a sign in the weight room stating that it is not a health club and that each person is using the equipment at his or her own risk (language to be copied from pages 5 and 6 of the Board packet) and after that, to ask Socher Insurance Agency if we can remove the defibrillator from the weight room.
