COMMITTEE REPORTS

 

Finance Committee Report:

The committee met on June 24 to go over the association’s finances. The committee will start the budgeting process in July. There are some cash flow concerns. Expenses remain stable but there are an increasing number of units going into collections and/or foreclosures.

The July meeting is scheduled for Tuesday, July 22 at 6:30 p.m. in the north clubhouse.

 

Office Report:

 

There has been no response to a letter that our legal counsel wrote in regards to an owner wanting to open an insurance claim against the association

 

The fountain between buildings 5 and 8 is not working. RPM Electric diagnosed a leak in the “conduit box” which will have to be replaced.

 

All the courtyard and parking lot lights on the south side were out for a couple of days. RPM installed a new contactor which fixed the problem.

 

We had to call the “haul away” service several times in the last month.

The Board asked the office to schedule our second “bulky item pick up” event with Waste Management for the second week in August. We are hoping that will stop residents from simply leaving big items by our dumpsters.

 

The Board asked the office to include the common area carpet cleaning in next month’s agenda.

 

There is still a “trouble on the line” signal periodically in Building 4’s fire alarm system.

 

OPEN FORUM:

 

A resident shared that her car is being vandalized by people trying to climb the back wall. The problem is that there are bushes all along that wall, but there is an empty area just in front of her parking space, making it the “ideal” spot for trespassers to jump the wall.

 

Motion:       Carl Kennedy made a motion to have our landscapers investigate the feasibility of installing  pyracantha all along the back wall (parallel to Cedar Blvd.) with the goal of having its thorns be a deterrent from trespassing.

2nd:            Joy Hurst

Vote:           All in favor: Carl Kennedy, Joy Hurst, Tim Young.  

Motion carried.

Ø        Water shut-off valve for clubhouses:

Neither clubhouse has its own water shut-off valve. If water needs to be shut-off for any reason in either clubhouse, all 4 buildings on that side would have to have their water shut-off as well. The Board directed the office to find out the cost of such a project and to include this item in next month’s meeting agenda.

Ø        Tree roots damaging neighbor’s patio:

The roots of the tree next to uncovered parking space # 265 have grown and have lifted the patio floor of the neighbors on the other side of the wall. The office will have additional conversations with Treeco to find a solution acceptable to all parties. The neighbors in question had been invited to attend the meeting. They will be invited again for the August meeting. This item is tabled until more information is available.
