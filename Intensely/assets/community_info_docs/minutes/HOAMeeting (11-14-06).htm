OPEN FORUM:

 

A resident was inquiring how she could register for the PEP class

 

Resident suggested that it would be a good idea to mention figures in the newsletter backing up the decision against earthquake insurance.

 

Resident pointed out that he has seen people throwing cigarette butts from the balconies. Board and attendees discussed different ways to stop the problem.

 

Grocery carts left inside the buildings continue to be a big concern. Non Foodmaxx grocery carts are not picked up by anyone and city dumpsters will not take them. Residents are strongly urged not to bring them into the complex at all.

 

 

 

COMMITTEE REPORTS

 

 

Office Report:

 

The Selcom system (Front door directories) works with cell phones with 510 area codes It will not always work with VOIP phone lines.

 

Maintenance supplies bought in a regular basis need to be approved by the Board if over $500.

 

The 2nd draft of the new governing documents is approximately 80 pages long. As soon as the Board gives the OK, it will be sent to all owners for their vote. Expense for this mailing will be high.

 

 

Finance Committee Report:

 

Last meeting was held on Tuesday, October 24. The committee revised the financials and approved the budget for 2007.  The association will not have earthquake insurance during 2007.

 

Next meeting will be held on Tuesday Nov. 21st, 2006 at 6:30 p.m. in the north clubhouse.

Ø        Fremont Fence Proposals for New Fences:
