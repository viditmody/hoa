COMMITTEE REPORTS

 

Finance Committee Report:

The committee met on November 25 to go over the association’s finances. The cash flow budget for 2009 is still not ready. The committee hopes to have it completed by the next meeting. The December meeting is scheduled for Tuesday, December 23 at 6:30 p.m. in the north clubhouse.

 

Manager’s Report:

 

The north spa was resurfaced with a fiberglass coating.

 

Lewis Carpet Cleaners professionally cleaned the common area carpets in Bldgs. 7 and 8.

 

All Seasons Roofing was here to perform the semi-annual roof maintenance for both sides of the complex.

 

The Bulky Item dumpster was very successful again. It is very full. Waste management is coming tomorrow to retrieve it.

 

The office knows of 10 cars broken into since October 1. The Newark Police Department said they will try to patrol Murieta more often.

 

The painting project is under way. Building 5 was power washed and painted. Ekim  Painting will power wash Building 6 tomorrow.

 

The multi purpose weight machine in the gym was delivered and the old one was hauled away.

Ø        CFL bulbs in lobby chandeliers :

Per the CFL bulbs manufacturer one chandelier in building 8 was modified to let heat escape hoping that this alteration would make the bulbs last longer. One of these bulbs burned in November failing the 3 month test. Murieta purchased 378 bulbs in February @ $11.95 each with a 5 year warranty. We have returned 140 burned bulbs so far.

 

Motion:       Carl Kennedy made a motion to uninstall all CFL bulbs from the chandeliers, return them to Batteries and Bulbs for a refund or credit and use that refund or credit towards the purchase of incandescent bulbs.

2nd:            Joy Hurst

Vote:           All in favor: May McMann, Carl Kennedy, Tim Young, Joy Hurst.

Motion carried.

 

Update on Painting Project:

 

Some residents have not been doing their part by emptying and/or moving items from their patios/balconies. We are starting to think that our method of posting signs in all building doors as our way to communicate with residents is not effective.

Ekim Painting offered to provide “door hangers” explaining exactly what each unit  needs to do to be prepared. Moving forward, the office will post signs on every unit door hoping that this will vastly improve resident awareness and cooperation. The units identified as not being prepared, will be assessed.
