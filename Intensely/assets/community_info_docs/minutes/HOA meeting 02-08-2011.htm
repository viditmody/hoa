Elastomeric roof coating (fluid applied roof system presentation) with Brad Meyer of All Seasons Roofing and Rod Fickel:

 

Our current roof was installed 10 years ago on top of the original roof. The life expectancy of the roof is 16 years. In order to install a new roof, we would first need to remove the two previous layers of roofing. Instead of going through the very costly and environmentally damaging process of installing a new roof, we now have the option of coating the existing roof with a “elastromeric coating” which is a reflective roof coating. This coating would have to be reapplied every 5 or 10 years (depending on the product we would choose) and it would make the existing roof last indefinitely.

This system would cost around $300,000 for a 5 year application or $350,000 for a 10 year application. Re-doing the whole roof with the existing kind of material would be about $1,000,000 plus all the inconvenience to the 3rd floor residents.

 

The Board asked All Seasons Roofing to provide  a quote with exact numbers on how much this project would cost for Murieta.
