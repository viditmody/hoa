Serpico Landscaping Co. Presentation:

Rudy Olivera and Ramon Gonzales from Serpico Landscaping attended the meeting to answer some questions from residents…

 

The irrigation system works by taking historical local weather information and reacting from that information. It ‘learns” what to expect over time. Sometimes, it takes a whole season to predict the weather patterns in the area.

Serpico is “fine tuning” the system right now. From time to time the system will need to be manually changed in order to reduce the window of time watering. The irrigation technicians will keep monitoring the system and making changes when needed.

 

A resident commented that the “‘birds of paradise” don’t look good. The Serpico reps. explained that such plants are trimmed in the winter, never when they are blooming because it could damage the health of the plant or it could even die.  They did acknowledge that there are lots of snails on them and they promised to apply extra “snail bait” to alleviate the situation. They clarified that because they use pet friendly products, it might not be the most effective at killing all snails immediately.

A resident brought up the fact that some plants by Building 6 were very dry and that the water doesn’t reach them. Serpico will look into it and try to adjust the sprinklers. If it is not possible or advisable for the sprinklers to reach that far back, they will propose to relocate the plants on a case by case basis.

 

Serpico will come up with a proposal regarding the areas next to the glass doors in both buildings 5 and 8 to strongly discourage pet waste.

 

 

COMMITTEE REPORTS

 

Finance Committee:

 

The committee met in April. Murieta seems to be in decent shape. The “belt tightening” seems to be paying off. The committee is worried though, about the amount of foreclosures and the potential of more coming. The amount for “bad debt” was under budgeted. So far this year, we have written off almost as much as we did in all of 2010 due to foreclosures. For the remaining of the year, it is the committee’s recommendation to continue approving health and safety items only.

The next meeting will take place on May 31 at 6:30 p.m. in the north clubhouse and everyone is invited to attend.

 

Manager’s Report:

 

We are in the process of moving towards using less paper and more e-mail. We offered all owners and residents to option to receive their monthly statements and newsletters by e-mail as a PDF file. Carl Street is helping us set up Constant Contact.

 

Residents are tampering with locks on the gates to prevent them from locking. We ask that everyone double check that the building doors and gates close and lock properly behind them. If they are not, please check for tape, little twigs or whatever is stuck in the lock and remove it.

 

Carl Street, long-time owner and former board member, offered to volunteer his time to make over the Murieta HOA website since this is his area of expertise. The Board was very happy with the offer and will gladly take him up on it. This will probably be a six month project so there will be ample opportunity for board oversight.

 

The carport re-roofing project that was postponed last year is scheduled to begin on June 6 and it will take approximately 4 weeks. This year, All Seasons Roofing will be re-roofing carport structures 16 to 20 (behind Building 3, beside Buildings 3 and 4, and 2 structures in the center driveway. All affected residents will receive a letter from the office with more information.

 

Sparkle Pool & Spa turned the solar water heater on. It takes about a month of sunny weather for the heater to start making a difference in the temperature of the water at the swimming pools. The pool furniture will be taken out next Wednesday.

There was a flood from a third floor hot water heater in Building 3. The water heater in question was an original (from circa. 1983)!!!!

 

The four wheels and tires were stolen from a car by Building 8 and left on cinder blocks. The police came and took fingerprints. We can only hope that they catch whoever did this.
