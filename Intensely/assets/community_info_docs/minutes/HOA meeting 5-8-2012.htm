COMMITTEE REPORTS

 

Finance Committee Report:

The treasurer of the Board was not able to attend the April committee meeting.

The association’s finances appear to be in good order. The Fremont Bank reserve account is over the FDIC limit. The committee recommends to start repaying the loans made from reserves to operating.

The next meeting will take place on Tuesday, May 29, 2012 at 6:30 p.m. in the north clubhouse when we will start discussions on the 2013 operating budget. All members are welcome to attend.

 

 

 

Manager’s Report:

 

There was a very destructive water damage event in building one. A 3rd floor owner hired a “handy man” (no license). Water to the stack was shut off. A faucet was removed and had not been re-installed when the handyman  turned the water on again on his way out for lunch. The result was extensive damage to the neighbors under this unit and the common area. Restoration Management was called and the repairs have been made. Since this incident is due to obvious negligence, the HOA will bill back and recover all its costs.

 

We finally managed to get the 2011 financial statements completed with much help from Marilyn Bell. We are underwhelmed by our current CPA’s performance and seriously think we need to “shop” for a new one.

 

The dryer vent cleaning project will begin the last week of May.

 

As soon as the Board decides new positions for the 2012-2013 term, Susan will begin the process of changing names on all the bank accounts.

 

Maintenance manager, Antonio, suffered two slashed tires while parked in Murieta’s lot.

 

Sparkle Pool is turning the solar power on this week. The pool furniture will be out ready for the weekend.

 

We got a report that there were mice in a wing of Building 7, 3rd floor. It has been established that the “intruders” came to Murieta when a new resident brought boxes from storage. Alameda County vector control is inspecting and giving advice to the unit(s) involved to solve the problem.

 

We discovered an unauthorized rental in Bldg. 2. Board advised the office to send a CC&R violation to them immediately.

Open Forum:

 

An owner stated that he has seen a decline in the appearance of the complex. Balconies are full of “junk” and it is beginning to look very “run down”. The Board asked the residents present at the meeting to get involved by paying attention and reporting unauthorized items on patios and balconies. The office will write about it on the newsletter.  We need more volunteers to do walkthroughs.

 

An owner inquired about ramps around Murieta. Some buildings have them and some don’t, making it very difficult for those on a wheel chair or for strollers.
