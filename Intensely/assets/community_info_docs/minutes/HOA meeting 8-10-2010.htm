COMMITTEE REPORTS

 

 

The association is trying to find ways to economize and Carl Kennedy asked the office for recommendations on items in the budget which could be cut back or eliminated to help with the cash flow and to somewhat alleviate the increase in dues coming soon to pay for the center driveway gates. This will be discussed further in the next finance committee meeting.

 

Manager’s Report:

Janitor said that the grocery cart problem has not improved.

 

The south pool got vandalized (bottles in pool, broken tables and umbrellas).

 

With our insurance’s permission we removed the defibrillator from the gym. The fire department was complaining about false alarms.

 

We found two new promising drywall repair contractors.

 

According to the reserve analysis, we should re-key the complex next year. We ran into problems recently with Armor Locks because they own the “key code” and it took them more than a month to supply us with a shipment of “gates and doors” keys that we desperately needed. Re-keying and ending our relationship with this unreliable vendor is looking very attractive.

 

The office got a new computer.

 

The carpet cleaning contract with Mr. Unlimited ended. There is a new proposal to renew it. The residents seem very happy with his work.

 

 

Open Forum:

 

Residents voiced their suggestions on how we can cut on costs.

 

Some were not aware of the upcoming increase in dues for the next 24 months to repay the cost of the center driveway gates.

 

A resident pointed out that she has seen a possible broken sprinkler behind Building 8. The office will forward the information to Serpico.
