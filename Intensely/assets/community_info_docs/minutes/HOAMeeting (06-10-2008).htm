OPEN FORUM:

 

Resident inquired about the progress of the parking lot gates project.

 

COMMITTEE REPORTS

 

Finance Committee Report:

The committee met on May 20 to go over the association’s finances. The committee will start the budgeting process in July or August.

The June meeting is scheduled for Tuesday, June 17 at 6:30 p.m. in the north clubhouse.

 

Office Report:

Gate Installation update: Electrical installation is on hold until Dryco does the digging, filling the conduit and pouring asphalt back. This will all happen on the same day.

R&S Erection is waiting on permits from the City of Newark.

Some residents have asked if the gates will restrict pedestrian traffic.

 

CFL bulbs are failing. We have returned 25 so far. They are under warranty and we hope we just got a bad batch and the rest will do fine.

 

A PG&E representative came and did an audit of the pool areas. He will send us a report with recommendations on how to save energy in that sector of the complex.

 

There is still a “trouble on the line” signal periodically in building 4’s fire alarm system.

 

 

OLD BUSINESS

 

 

Ø        Report on Newark City Council meeting:

About 12 Murieta owners/residents attended the meeting. Fred Trudeau (owner who is an attorney and volunteered to help the association) did a great job. He was direct and stated the facts without being confrontational. John Becker, city manager, and the council seemed very receptive. The contract with Waste Management runs until 2012.

 

Ø        Television wiring upgrade:

To upgrade the wiring to allow 2 signals coming into each unit would require access in each and every unit in addition to the crawl space on top of 3rd floor.
